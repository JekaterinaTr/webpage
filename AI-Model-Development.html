AI Model Development & Optimization

Dense object detection, forecasting, and anomaly detection across agriculture, life sciences, and environmental applications. I design, train, and optimize robust AI systems that work in real-world conditions.

Example: Wheat Head Detection (Dense Small-Object Detection)

Detecting and counting wheat heads in highly dense field images is challenging due to small size, heavy overlap, and low contrast. This project demonstrates end-to-end AI model development: training, evaluation, and deployment-focused optimization.

Approach

Compared multiple architectures: YOLO11/12 (one-stage CNN), RF-DETR (transformer-based), and Cascade R-CNN (two-stage CNN).

Evaluation & Metrics

  • Detection: mAP@50, mAP@50–95
  • Counting: MAE, RMSE
  • Performance: FPS, GPU memory, model size
  • Robustness: performance on sparse vs dense scenes
